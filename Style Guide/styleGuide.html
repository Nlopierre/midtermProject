Debt Bidder Style Guide

This style guide is to be used as a reference to maintain visual consistency when creating new, different webpages for this website.

Strategy

Debt Bidder's purpose is to encourage good financial practices by facilitating tasks such as renegotiating a debt. Since we like to watch our customers grow, it is important for us to provide advices and tools that can help them plan and build a better tomorrow. To do that, Debt Bidder simplifies and streamlines processes that normally take days to accomplish, and creates a direct bridge between lender and borrower. As an organization focusing on taking care of financial responsibilities, we provide our customers with a safe and easy platform to manage their debts.

Code Conventions

Naming Conventions

When it comes to coding, we prefer to use words-with-dashes. DO NOT write them with capital cases. For example, when inserting an attribute to a link, do it this way:

---Avoid camelCase---

href="this-is-the-correct-way.html"

Attributes

Include the alt attribute for all images. Since we are targeting a very diverse group of people, we need to make sure that the Screen Readers can work with our websites nicely.

img alt="explain what the image is about" />

Copy

To highlight our pursue of simpleness, we want of tone of voice to be as direct and professional as possible.

Customers appreciate clarity. Therefore, always refer the customers to check terms and conditions when they are about to take action. Highlight the most important details of each action.

Given our diverse range of customers, it is important keep feeding the respective attributes to collaborate with Screen Readers. We want our clients to feel that they are all part of our mission so use language the welcomes them and invites them to share our vision.

Images

These types of images are the ones we would like to show our customers because they represent the values and the characteristics that we believe in.

Layouts and Grids

Following up, you will find the templates used to create the current pages of this website. Notice how only the input spaces and tables have right-angle corners and the buttons have round corners. The renegotiation button and negotiation preference button have to be present in all of the webpages (or as many as possible) since it is the option we want the customer to check out.
